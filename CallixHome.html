About Callix Electronics...

Founded in 1973 in Anneksia, Callix Electronics transformed the nation's technology industry with groundbreaking advancements in computing, gaming, and audio systems. The company made its mark with the Com-Callix line of computers, powered by the Callix Disk System Operating System (CdsOS), which simplified personal and business computing across Anneksia. Expanding into gaming, Callix introduced the Pixigear handheld console, bringing classic titles like Nova and Tanks to players. In 1993, the company launched the Vallrix 32X home system, featuring iconic games such as Xedyalla and Iron Campaign, further cementing its influence in the gaming market. Beyond computing and gaming, Callix revolutionized media storage and playback with the Callix-Multiuse-Protected-Disc, simplifying data management. The company also played a key role in popularizing the Gallian Cassette system in Anneksia, launching the Callix AR-100 music player and later the DB-9 Portable music system, making high-quality portable audio more accessible. With a legacy of pioneering innovation, Callix Electronics remains a leader in shaping the future of technology and entertainment in Anneksia and beyond.
